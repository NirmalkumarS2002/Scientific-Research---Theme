Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common enquiries we receive from partners, patients, and institutions.

Can't find your answer?

Our research liaison team handles complex enquiries daily. Send us a detailed message and we will connect you with the right expert within 24 hours.

How can our hospital partner with your research institute?

add

We actively seek clinical partners to participate in ongoing and upcoming trials. Submit a partnership enquiry via our contact form selecting "Research Partnership" and our collaborations team will reach out within two business days to discuss scope, ethics approvals, and data sharing agreements.

Can patients or individuals enrol directly in clinical trials?

add

Patient recruitment is managed through our registered hospital partners. Please speak with your physician and ask them to contact us on your behalf. We ensure every participant receives full informed consent documentation and ongoing medical support throughout any trial.

What laboratory testing services are available to external clients?

add

Our ISO-certified labs offer genomic sequencing, mass spectrometry, proteomics profiling, ELISA assays, and pharmaceutical stability testing. Send your requirements to research@pharmalab.com and we will provide a quote and turnaround timeline within 48 hours.

Do you accept international research collaboration proposals?

add

Absolutely. We currently maintain active collaborations with institutions across 12 countries. International proposals should be submitted to partnerships@pharmalab.com with a one-page brief, your institution's credentials, and a proposed scope of work.

How quickly will I receive a response to my enquiry?

add

All enquiries submitted through the contact form receive an automated acknowledgement immediately. A human response from the relevant department follows within one business day (Mon–Fri, 9 AM – 6 PM IST). Urgent clinical matters can call our direct line.

Are your research findings publicly accessible?

add

We are committed to open-access publishing. The majority of our completed studies are available via PubMed and our website's research library. Pre-publication summaries are shared exclusively with newsletter subscribers before wider release.
